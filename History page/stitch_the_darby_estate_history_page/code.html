The Story of
The Darby Estate

"For nearly a century, these walls have witnessed the soft descent of winter snow and the vibrant bloom of mountain summers."

Originally established in 1946 as The Darby Field Inn, this historic property was born from a vision of mountain sanctuary. Nestled in the heart of Albany, New Hampshire, it served for generations as a beacon for travelers seeking the rugged beauty of the White Mountains and the warmth of a true colonial hearth.

The transformation into The Darby Estate was a labor of preservation. We didn't just renovate; we curated. Every original timber, the grand fieldstone fireplaces, and the sprawling gardens were meticulously restored to maintain their "archival warmth"—that elusive feeling of a home that has been lived in, loved, and passed down.

Today, we offer what modern hotels cannot: exclusive stewardship. When you walk through our doors, you aren't just a guest; you are the master of a grand private sanctuary, inheriting the legacy of 1946 for your own private celebration.
