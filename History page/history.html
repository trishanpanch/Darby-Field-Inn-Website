The Story of
The Darby Field Inn

Originally built in 1826, this historic property was born from a vision of being a mountain sanctuary.

Nestled in the heart of Albany, New Hampshire, The Darby Field Inn was privately owned until 1946, when it then served for generations as a beacon for travelers seeking the rugged beauty of the White Mountains and the warmth of a true colonial homestead.

Since then, the transformation into The Darby Field Inn was a labor of preservation. Every original timber, the grand fieldstone fireplaces, and the sprawling gardens were restored to maintain their "archival warmth"—the elusive feeling of a home that has been lived in, loved, and passed down for many years.
